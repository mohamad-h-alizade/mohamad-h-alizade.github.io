I am an Electrical Engineer. I graduated from Sharif University of Technology (SUT) in 2022 with a focus on data-driven models and signal processing. Since then I am taking graduate-level courses at University of Tehran. After completing my bachelor's degree, I worked with a group at University of Southern Denmark (SDU) for six months. Supervised by Prof. Manouchehr Vafaee, I processed brain signals, which sparked my interest in graph-based models. Since then, I collaborated with a research group led by Prof. Jhony H. Giraldo at LTCI, Télécom Paris, Institut Polytechnique de Paris. Here, I worked on incorporating node-specific information in the clustering of graph signals. You can read my CV here.

Research: I work in the areas of graph learning and information processing. In the emerging field of graph signal processing (GSP), graph learning is the inference of a graph's topology from observations on its nodes, i.e. graph signals. My research aims to unravel hidden relationships among data entities, which in return increases the robustness and explainability of downstream models. My current research centers on incorporating node-side information into the clustering of graph signals. I am also broadly interested in kernel methods and the role of tensor decompositions in dimensionality reduction.
